Hi, I’m Mig Reyes, a web designer from Chicago working at Basecamp.

Making

I help make and market Basecamp, the world’s best project management software. Before joining Basecamp (formerly 37signals), I designed for the web as Threadless’s first full-time interactive designer. With my roots in traditional graphic arts, I experiment outside of web and product work as well.

Doing

I volunteer a large portion of my time to community outreach. Since 2009, I’ve been teaching art direction and design within Chicago portfolio and tech schools. I also maintain Humble Pied, a video blog where I interview the design and tech industry’s most influential people.

Writing

I’m trying to be a better writer everyday. I like to write about design, craft, and culture. You can read some of my favorite pieces so far: Try, try again, When culture turns to policy, and Reminder: Design is still about words.

Speaking

Every now and again I travel to give presentations on creativity and design. My last favorite talk was the one I gave at CreativeMornings. It’s four pieces of backwards advice that captures how I feel on the way we live our lives as creators.
